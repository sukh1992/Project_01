In conversation with John Mcdonald

An interview by Sukhninder Singh

Sukh— What was considered "beautiful" or "handsome" when you were my age?

JOHN—In the young age when I see advertisement on the T.V then I thought the muscular body or glowing face are the keys of beautiful or handsome but with the time I realise one should have beautiful from inside of the body not from the outside.

Sukh—Was your self-esteem affected by your size or shape as you were growing up?

John—I was very skinny in young age, but my self-esteem did not affect by my size because my parents were always with me and they never let me down.

Sukh—Did you engage in any "practices" (special diets, exercise, use of supplements, etc.) to alter your body shape in order to meet the standard "ideal" of physical beauty?

John—Yes, I joined gym to become muscular, but I did not get what I wanted. I joined gym not because of people or for anybody. I joined gym just for myself.

Sukh—How much time, money, and energy did you devote to making yourself "beautiful" / "handsome" when you were my age?

John—I did not use lot of products to look handsome, but I spent some money on hair gel and moisturising cream.

Sukh— What were the primary body image influences in your life when you were my age? (friends, family, media, magazines, sports, other)

JOHN—My primary influence was sports because I had great interest in sports, and I wanted to stay fit always so that my body not become obstacle in my game.

Sukh— What 2 to 4 suggestions do you have for achieving a healthy and happy body image at any age?

JOHN—The first thing I want to say believe yourself. Second, please join gym or do some exercise, not to become muscular or sexy just to stay fit and stay away from diseases. Last but not least that be happy with your body whatever you have.

Sukh— If your body could talk, what would it say?

JOHN—It will say to me that “whether you are skinny, but you are handsome because you love your body so much.

Sukh— What helps you deal with stress in a healthy way?

JOHN—I use different ways to stress out myself but the main way for me is sport. Whenever I feel stress, I always try to take part in outdoor games.

Sukh— What are your favourite ways to move your body?

JOHN—As I said before my favourite thing is sport and that is my favourite way to move my body

Sukh— How do you define 'beautiful" in a woman and "handsome" in a man?

JOHN—I say beautiful or handsome to a woman or man who is not beautiful or handsome from outside of the body but should be from inside

In conversation with Charan Kaur

An interview by Sukhninder Singh

Sukh— What was considered "beautiful" or "handsome" when you were my age?

Charan—At that time beauty was all about behaviour. Especially for me because my parents taught me in very young age that the person who has great nature and behaviour is always beautiful or handsome.

Sukh—Was your self-esteem affected by your size or shape as you were growing up?

Charan—Never because I had enough confidence at that time, body or shape never matter for me. According to me If someone has confidence that I am perfect then it always perfect and I had that confidence.

Sukh—Did you engage in any "practices" (special diets, exercise, use of supplements, etc.) to alter your body shape in order to meet the standard "ideal" of physical beauty?

Charan—No because when I was young there were no gyms or exercise places. We didn’t have televisions, mobiles or computers at that time, so we were engaged in physical activities like sports, work in fields, etc. and I think we were more healthy or fit than this generation because of these activities.

Sukh—How much time, money, and energy did you devote to making yourself "beautiful" / "handsome" when you were my age?

Charan—I spent very small amount of money on me when I was young. I used to be ready only when I had to go to a function or parties.

Sukh— What were the primary body image influences in your life when you were my age? (friends, family, media, magazines, sports, other)

Charan—At that time there was not such a special effect on me, but yes, I used to feel very much with the body shape of a Bollywood actress named ‘Madhubala’

Sukh— What 2 to 4 suggestions do you have for achieving a healthy and happy body image at any age?

Charan—The main thing is that you do maximum housework. Avoid fast food and keep stress as far away from you as possible.

Sukh— If your body could talk, what would it say?

Charan—She will tell me that your body is healthy and keep it maintained with a workout and now also teach the children of today’s generation how you can get a good body without using any modern product.

Sukh— What helps you deal with stress in a healthy way?

Charan—According to me, talking with someone is a very good solution to get stressed out. Whenever you feel that you are in stress, then you should talk to someone who can understand you.

Sukh— What are your favourite ways to move your body?

Charan—The best way is to do household chores and if possible, visit in the evening or morning.I maintained my body only by this way.

Sukh— How do you define 'beautiful" in a woman and "handsome" in a man?

Charan—It depends on everything that you do. How do you treat others? I agree that it is important to look beautiful from outside, but your behavior matter the most for both Men and Women.
